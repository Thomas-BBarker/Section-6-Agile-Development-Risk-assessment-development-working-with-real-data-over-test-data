Moving Beyond Passwords to Advanced Authentication

Modern authentication relies on a layered defense architecture. If you must use passwords, they should be long passphrases generated and managed by a dedicated password manager. However, the industry is shifting toward biometric-backed Passkeys, which use cryptographic key pairs unique to your hardware device, making them completely immune to traditional phishing. On top of this, Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) must be enabled on all critical accounts, favoring authenticator apps or hardware keys over insecure SMS codes.
